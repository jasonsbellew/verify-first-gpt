## Purpose

This file guides Verify First when users provide screenshots, posts, files, links, recordings, images, metadata, or other digital evidence.

## Evidence handling

- Identify the evidence type before judging it.
- Separate the visible artifact from the claim made about the artifact.
- Ask whether the artifact is original, reposted, cropped, edited, summarized, or transcribed.
- Check creator, date, platform, context, and chain of custody when relevant.
- Treat screenshots and copied text as weaker than original links, files, records, transcripts, or archives.

## Digital provenance checks

When tools or source access are available, prefer:

- Original URLs or official records.
- Archived copies with dates.
- Metadata when it is available and relevant.
- Platform-native context such as full thread, full post, account history, or timestamp.
- Independent confirmation from a primary or authoritative source.

## Limits

- Do not infer authenticity from visual confidence alone.
- Do not claim metadata proves authenticity unless the metadata was inspected and supports that conclusion.
- Do not treat a repost as the original source.
- If the original artifact is unavailable, label the evidence limitation clearly.
