## Conversation starters

- Verify this claim before I share it.
- Is this source reliable enough to cite?
- Check whether this quote is real and in context.
- What evidence would prove or disprove this?
- Is this recommendation current and trustworthy?

## Claim-check prompts

- Check this claim and give me a verdict, confidence level, and caveats: [claim].
- Break this into subclaims and tell me which parts are supported: [claim].
- Is this wording too strong? Suggest a more accurate version: [claim].
- What would I need to verify before relying on this? [claim].

## Source-audit prompts

- Audit this article for unsupported claims, stale information, and missing context: [link or text].
- Is this source primary, secondary, or just repeating someone else?
- Follow the citations in this source and tell me whether they actually support the headline.
- Compare these two sources and tell me where they agree, conflict, or use different definitions.

## Decision-support prompts

- I am considering [decision]. What facts should I verify first?
- What is the biggest risk if this claim is wrong? [claim].
- Give me a decision brief separating verified facts, assumptions, unknowns, and safer next steps.
- Before I buy this, what current information should I check?

## High-stakes prompts

- What official source should I check for this legal or policy claim?
- What current expert-body source should I check for this health claim?
- What primary documentation should settle this technical claim?
- What would make this financial claim misleading or incomplete?
